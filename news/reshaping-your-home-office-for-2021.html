Reshaping your home office for 2021

Jan 14 2021

Time to upgrade from the laptop sloucher to a home office hero

We spent most of 2020 adapting. Now we’re in 2021, it’s time to take what we learned and excel. Our resident smart meeting expert Simon Kitson has all the latest tech suggestions for 2021.

Over the last 12 months, whether intentionally or not, we’ve all found our own little niches; individual ways of maximizing our productivity and ensuring that we’re being the best we can be from our hybrid work spaces.

It’s your house, your rules. So naturally, on these matters the user knows best. But when it comes to cold hard tech, there are some great new innovations to check out as we sink our teeth firmly into 2021.

Logitech Workspace Configurator

If you’re short of time and want to have everything worked out for you then you should absolutely make use of the Logitech Workspace configurator.

In around 30 seconds this nifty little programme will recommend an optimum workspace set up, based on your own needs and requirements. It factors in elements such as productivity needs, posture, comfort and aesthetic - even your hand size - to generate a bespoke solution that will work the best for you.

Premium Image Quality

Poor video and sub-par image is a massive detractor from the quality of your meetings, a good camera is your window to the working world, so it needs to be up to scratch. Here you’ve got a lot of options at your disposal. For a truly premium image, the Huddly Go is the stand out candidate. Created for home working and huddle spaces it is one of the most dynamic VC cameras on the market today.

Another great alternative is the new ThinkSmart Cam from Lenovo. It can be plugged into your own device for home office, or when paired with a compute, such as ThinkSmart Hub or ThinkSmart Edition Tiny, it transforms meeting rooms into immersive, productive spaces. ThinkSmart Cam is certified for compatibility with both Microsoft Teams Rooms and Zoom Rooms platforms.

Know your kit

Investing in good quality VC kit is one thing, but you need to know its functions and more importantly its limitations. If you’re a little unsure then speaking to our teams will get you started on the right path.

For instance, did you know that if you’re a Zoom user you can drastically improve your sound quality by selecting the “Enable Original Sound” function. Teams, Google Meet and Zoom also all have options to enable HD quality on your camera, so if you have the bandwidth, then make sure that box is ticked.

The last thing to focus on is bringing in a wired network connection into your workspace. This will vastly improve your virtual working environment. It will improve your overall stability across all online work, and you’ll find you have clearer sound and vision.

Microsoft Surface Hub for home

Dynamism is the name of the game in the hybrid world, and Microsoft’s flagship collaboration device the Surface Hub 2S has continued to excel in this area. It can be used as a VC device, bringing in a collaborative whiteboard to enable simultaneous teamwork which is as close as being in the office together as it’s possible to be.

With coordinated join your participants can view the whiteboard through your camera and directly in the call. Plus if you are using Teams everyone can contribute and edit the whiteboard in real time. The 55” model is the perfect size moving between the home and work office and requires no extra setup beyond putting on a stand and switching on. Premier Audio Quality

Hand in hand with image quality is sound quality. The best camera in the world means next to nothing if your teams can’t understand a word you’re saying.

Audio can be a little tricker to recommend off the shelf - there is a lot of dependency on the space you’re in and how that affects sound quality - you can still make some investments into quality kit that’ll make you heard.

Two of the most common problems with audio are that background noise is drawing you out, or you just aren’t being heard enough. To fix the former and the latter, you might want to consider a headset such as the Logitech Zone.

A wireless bluetooth headset designed with VC in mind, battery and talk time are incredible and the mic array picks up the clear sound of your voice. It also features flip-to-mute, so should you need to go on mute, that functionality is right there on the headset.

If you’re not a headset kind of person another option would be something like the Logitech Brio. A marvellous piece of VC kit which combines the best of both quality image and sound, in one small yet powerful camera that is fully Teams, Zoom and Google certified.
